Mud Road Construction

Mud roads, often rural or temporary, are essential for connecting remote areas. These roads are generally made with minimal preparation and materials, often utilizing the natural terrain. Typically consist of compacted earth, gravel, and local aggregates.

Highway Road Construction

Highways are engineered for high-speed, long-distance travel, and must be designed for durability, safety, and efficient traffic flow. Use of multiple layers including sub-base (sand, gravel), base course (crushed stone or asphalt), and surface course (concrete or asphalt).

Pipeline Construction

Pipelines are used for transporting fluids like water, oil, and gas over long distances, requiring careful planning to ensure safety and efficiency. Typically steel, PVC, or high-density polyethylene (HDPE) pipes depending on the material being transported.
